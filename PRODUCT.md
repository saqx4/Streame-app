# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Cord-cutters and media enthusiasts who want a full-featured streaming client, plus a broader audience who just wants a smooth viewing experience without technical friction. Core users manage their own content sources via Stremio addons, TorrServer, or CloudStream. Newer users may start with defaults and explore advanced options over time. Primary job: browse catalogs and watch without friction, regardless of technical depth.

## Product Purpose

A Flutter-based streaming app that serves as a cross-platform client (Android TV, mobile, desktop) for Stremio addon ecosystem. Users browse TMDB-powered catalogs, maintain watchlists, track progress across devices via Supabase sync, and watch via multiple streaming sources. Success = seamless content discovery and playback.

## Brand Personality

**Refined utility** - Function-first with polish where it matters. Cinematic backdrops and smooth transitions serve the content, not decoration. Every pixel earns its place, but the experience feels considered, not raw. Think precision tool with a dark, quiet confidence.

## Anti-references

- Generic streaming (Netflix/Disney+: heavy gradients, dense posters, carousel-overload)
- SaaS-cream (light backgrounds, excessive rounded corners, rounded-everything)
- AI-clean (minimalist with massive whitespace, sparse interfaces)
- Cluttered TV UIs with too many visible options at once
- Stremio's own UI (cluttered, inconsistent, web-first, doesn't feel native on TV)
- Generic Material Design (default blue/purple, obvious M3 components, no personality)

## Design Principles

1. **Content-first** - Posters and backdrops carry the visual weight. UI chrome is dark and subtle.
2. **Performance is a feature** - Fast navigation, instant focus response. No layout shifts.
3. **Function over form** - Every setting and option is accessible. Customization is a feature.
4. **TV-native** - Focus rings, D-pad navigation, proper 10-foot UX with correct tap targets.
5. **Dark-by-default** - Optimized for dim living rooms, not bright offices.
6. **Platform-appropriate** - TV, mobile, and desktop each get layouts tuned to their input method and viewing distance, not just responsive breakpoints.
7. **Quiet confidence** - The UI doesn't announce itself. No flashy transitions or decorative elements. Refined restraint.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- High contrast focus rings for TV navigation
- Support for system-level subtitle rendering
- Reduced motion consideration for animations
- No reliance on color alone for state (accompanying icons/labels)
